# Introduction:

Pork butt roast, despite its misleading name, is a cut known for its tenderness, rich flavor, and versatility in the kitchen. Whether roasted, slow-cooked, or smoked, this cut of pork yields succulent and juicy results that are sure to delight your taste buds. In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore everything you need to know to master the art of cooking a pork butt roast, from selecting the perfect cut to seasoning and cooking techniques that will ensure a mouthwatering meal every time.

Choosing the Right Cut:

Despite its name, pork butt roast comes from the shoulder of the pig, not the rear. This cut is also known as pork shoulder or Boston butt. When selecting a pork butt roast, look for a well-marbled piece with a good balance of fat and meat. A bone-in roast will typically yield more flavor, but boneless roasts are easier to carve.

# Ingredients:

- 1 pork butt roast (4-6 pounds)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 1 teaspoon black pepper
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
- 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth (for roasting)
- Optional: additional herbs and spices, barbecue sauce, or glaze for extra flavor

Instructions:

1\. Preparing the Pork Butt Roast:

   - Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C).

   - Remove the pork butt roast from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes to take the chill off, which will help it cook more evenly.

   - Pat the roast dry with paper towels to remove any excess moisture.

2\. Seasoning the Roast:

   - In a small bowl, combine the salt, black pepper, garlic powder, paprika, dried thyme, and dried rosemary to create a flavorful dry rub.

   - Rub the olive oil all over the surface of the pork butt roast, then sprinkle the dry rub evenly over the entire roast, massaging it into the meat to ensure it adheres well.

3\. Roasting the Pork Butt:

   - Place the seasoned pork butt roast in a roasting pan or on a wire rack set inside a baking sheet.

   - Pour the chicken or vegetable broth into the bottom of the roasting pan to help keep the roast moist during cooking.

   - Cover the roasting pan loosely with aluminum foil, ensuring that it's tented to allow for airflow.

   - Roast the pork butt in the preheated oven for approximately 3-4 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 195-200°F (90-93°C) and the meat is tender and easily shreds with a fork.

4\. Resting and Serving:

   - Once the pork butt roast is cooked through, remove it from the oven and tent it loosely with foil to rest for about 20-30 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, resulting in a juicier roast.

   - After resting, use two forks to shred the pork into bite-sized pieces. Serve the shredded pork butt roast hot, either as is or with your favorite barbecue sauce, glaze, or gravy.

### Tips for Success:

- For added flavor, marinate the pork butt roast overnight in your favorite marinade or brine before seasoning and cooking.
- Experiment with different herbs and spices in the dry rub to customize the flavor of your roast.
- Consider using a meat thermometer to ensure that the pork butt roast reaches the desired internal temperature for optimal doneness.
- Leftover shredded pork butt roast can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 3-4 days or frozen for up to 3 months.
