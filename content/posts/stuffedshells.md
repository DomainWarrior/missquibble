# Introduction:

Stuffed shells are a classic Italian-American dish that never fails to impress. With their tender pasta shells filled with a savory mixture of cheese, herbs, and sometimes meat, they are a comforting and satisfying meal. Whether you're cooking for a family dinner or entertaining guests, stuffed shells are sure to be a hit. In this article, we'll walk you through a step-by-step guide on how to make these delicious stuffed shells right in your own kitchen.

## Ingredients:

To make stuffed shells, you will need the following ingredients:

- 1 box (12-16 ounces) of jumbo pasta shells
- 1 pound of ricotta cheese
- 1 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese
- 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
- 1 egg, lightly beaten
- 2 cloves of garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon of fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 cups of marinara sauce
- Optional: cooked ground meat such as beef, turkey, or sausage

### Instructions:

1\. Cook the Pasta Shells:

   - Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.

   - Add the jumbo pasta shells and cook according to the package instructions until al dente.

   - Drain the shells and rinse them under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.

2\. Prepare the Filling:

   - In a large mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, shredded mozzarella cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, beaten egg, minced garlic, chopped parsley, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.

   - If using cooked ground meat, add it to the cheese mixture and mix until well combined.

3\. Stuff the Shells:

   - Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

   - Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.

   - Take each cooked pasta shell and fill it generously with the cheese mixture, using a spoon or your hands to stuff it firmly.

   - Place the stuffed shells in the baking dish in a single layer.

4\. Bake the Stuffed Shells:

   - Once all the shells are filled and arranged in the baking dish, spoon the remaining marinara sauce over the top, covering the shells evenly.

   - Sprinkle additional shredded mozzarella cheese and grated Parmesan cheese on top, if desired.

   - Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

5\. Serve and Enjoy:

   - Once the stuffed shells are done baking, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes.

   - Garnish with fresh parsley or basil leaves, if desired, before serving.

   - Serve the stuffed shells hot, accompanied by a side salad and garlic bread for a complete meal.

# Conclusion:

Making stuffed shells at home is easier than you might think, and the results are absolutely delicious. With a few simple ingredients and some basic kitchen skills, you can create a hearty and satisfying dish that will impress your family and friends. Whether you stick to the classic cheese filling or get creative with added meat or vegetables, stuffed shells are sure to become a favorite in your recipe repertoire. So roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and get ready to enjoy a mouthwatering meal of homemade stuffed shells!
