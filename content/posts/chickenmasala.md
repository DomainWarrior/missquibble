# Introduction:

Chicken Masala, a beloved dish from the Indian subcontinent, is a testament to the rich tapestry of flavors and spices that characterize Indian cuisine. This aromatic and spicy dish features tender chicken cooked in a luxurious blend of tomatoes, onions, garlic, ginger, and a medley of spices. While it may seem intimidating to replicate the authentic flavors of Chicken Masala at home, fear not, as we'll guide you through a step-by-step process to create this culinary masterpiece in your own kitchen. Get ready to embark on a flavorful journey as we unlock the secrets of making Chicken Masala from scratch.

## Ingredients:

To make Chicken Masala, you'll need the following ingredients:

For Marinating the Chicken:

- 1 pound (450g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1/2 cup plain yogurt
- 1 tablespoon ginger paste
- 1 tablespoon garlic paste
- 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
- Salt to taste

For the Masala Sauce:

- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee (clarified butter)
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
- 2 teaspoons ginger paste
- 2 teaspoons garlic paste
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1 teaspoon garam masala
- 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
- 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
- Salt to taste
- 1/2 cup water
- Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)

# Instructions:

1\. Marinate the Chicken:

   - In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger paste, garlic paste, ground turmeric, ground cumin, ground coriander, red chili powder, and salt.

   - Mix well to ensure the chicken is evenly coated with the marinade. Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator for maximum flavor.

2\. Prepare the Masala Sauce:

   - Heat vegetable oil or ghee in a large skillet or pan over medium heat.

   - Add the chopped onion and sauté until it turns golden brown and translucent, about 5-7 minutes.

   - Stir in the ginger paste and garlic paste, and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

   - Add the chopped tomatoes to the skillet and cook until they soften and break down, forming a thick paste, about 5-7 minutes.

3\. Cook the Chicken:

   - Once the masala base is ready, add the marinated chicken pieces along with any remaining marinade to the skillet.

   - Cook the chicken over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally, until it is cooked through and tender, about 10-12 minutes.

4\. Season the Masala Sauce:

   - Add ground cumin, ground coriander, garam masala, ground turmeric, red chili powder, and salt to taste to the skillet.

   - Stir well to combine, ensuring that the chicken is coated evenly with the spices.

5\. Simmer and Serve:

   - Pour water into the skillet to adjust the consistency of the masala sauce, and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.

   - Let the Chicken Masala simmer uncovered for an additional 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together and the sauce to thicken slightly.

   - Once the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has reached the desired consistency, remove the skillet from heat.

   - Garnish the Chicken Masala with freshly chopped cilantro leaves for a burst of freshness and color.

6\. Serve and Enjoy:

   - Serve the Chicken Masala hot, accompanied by steamed rice, naan bread, or roti.

   - Enjoy the delightful fusion of spices and flavors in every bite of this classic Indian dish.
